<p><p>Magnetoresistive heads are classified based on their structure and design into two main types: Film Type Magnetoresistive Heads and Needle Type Magnetoresistive Heads. Film type heads consist of thin films of magnetic and non-magnetic materials, while needle type heads have a needle-like design with a magnetic tip. Both types are used in various applications such as hard disk drives and magnetic sensors. The market for these heads is driven by the demand for high storage capacity and data transfer rates in electronic devices.</p></p>
