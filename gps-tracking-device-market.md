<p><p>GPS tracking devices can be categorized into two main types: satellite-based devices and cellular-based devices. Satellite-based devices rely on a network of satellites to determine the location of an object or person in real-time. These devices are typically used in remote areas where cellular coverage is limited. Conversely, cellular-based devices utilize cellular networks to track the location of an object or person. These devices are commonly used in urban and populated areas where cellular coverage is reliable and widespread.</p></p>
