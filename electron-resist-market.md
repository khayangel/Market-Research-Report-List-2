<p><p>Electron resist refers to a type of material used in electron beam lithography for creating patterns on surfaces. Positive electron resist becomes more soluble when exposed to electron beams, while negative electron resist becomes less soluble. Positive electron resist is commonly used for high-resolution patterning, while negative electron resist is preferred for producing larger features. Both types of electron resist have specific applications in the semiconductor industry, with positive resist being favored for its high sensitivity and resolution, and negative resist for its extended shelf life and robustness.</p></p>
