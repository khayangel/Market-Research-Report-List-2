<p><p>Isoparaffin solvents are classified based on their carbon chain length, with C8, C12, C16, C20, and other types available in the market. Isoparaffins with eight carbon atoms (C8) are lighter and have low viscosity, making them suitable for applications like inks and paints. C12 isoparaffins have a balance of solvency and evaporation rate, making them ideal for cleaning products. C16 isoparaffins are commonly used in cosmetics and personal care products. C20 isoparaffins are heavier and find applications in lubricants and industrial cleaning. Other types of isoparaffins cater to specific industry needs.</p></p>
