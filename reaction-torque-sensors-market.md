<p><p>Reaction torque sensors come in various types, including Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW), Optical, Strain Gauge, and Magnetoelastic. SAW sensors utilize acoustic waves to measure torque, while Optical sensors use light to detect torque changes. Strain Gauge sensors use electrical resistance changes, and Magnetoelastic sensors rely on magnetic properties to measure torque. Each type offers unique advantages and applications, catering to different industry needs and requirements for accurate torque measurement.</p></p>
