<p><ul><li>RC(Regular Chain)</li><li>FC(Franchise Chain)</li><li>VC(Voluntary Chain)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Pharmaceutical retail chains can be categorized into three main types: RC (Regular Chain), FC (Franchise Chain), and VC (Voluntary Chain). Regular chains are owned and operated by a single entity, providing consistency in branding and operations. Franchise chains consist of independently owned stores that operate under a common brand and business model. Voluntary chains are groups of independent pharmacies that unite for collective purchasing power and marketing strategies while retaining their autonomy. Each type offers different advantages and opportunities for growth in the pharmaceutical retail market.</p></p>
