<p><p>Mechanical duplicators rely on a stencil that is cut by hand and then placed on a drum for reproduction. They are primarily used for duplicating simple text and line drawings. Digital duplicators, on the other hand, use digital technology to scan and reproduce documents quickly and with high quality. These machines are capable of reproducing a wide range of colors and images, making them more versatile than mechanical duplicators. Both types of duplicators serve the purpose of making multiple copies of documents efficiently.</p></p>
