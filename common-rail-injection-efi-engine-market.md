<p><p>Common-rail injection EFI engines are widely used in the automotive industry, with two common types being SDI (Solenoid Direct Injection) and TDI (Turbocharged Direct Injection). SDI engines use solenoid valves to control fuel delivery, while TDI engines incorporate turbocharging for increased power and efficiency. Both types offer improved fuel efficiency, reduced emissions, and enhanced performance compared to traditional fuel injection systems. These engines are popular choices for modern vehicles due to their advanced technology and ability to meet strict environmental regulations.</p></p>
