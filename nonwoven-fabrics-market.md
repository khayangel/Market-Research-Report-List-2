<p><p>Nonwoven fabrics can be categorized into different types based on the manufacturing process. Dry-laid nonwovens are made using mechanical or chemical bonding of fibers, while spunmelt nonwovens are produced by simultaneously spinning and bonding fibers. Wet-laid nonwovens involve forming a web from fibers in a water suspension, and others include methods like needlepunch and stitchbond. Each type of nonwoven fabric has unique characteristics and applications, catering to various industries such as healthcare, hygiene, construction, and automotive.</p></p>
