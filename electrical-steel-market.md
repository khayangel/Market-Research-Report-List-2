<p><p>Electrical steel is classified into two main types based on the orientation of the electrical and magnetic properties: grain-oriented and non-grain-oriented. Grain-oriented electrical steel is specifically designed for use in transformers and other power distribution applications due to its superior magnetic properties. Non-grain-oriented electrical steel, on the other hand, is used in motors, generators, and other rotating machinery where magnetic properties are important but not as critical as in transformers. Both types are essential in various electrical and electronic devices to ensure efficient energy conversion.</p></p>
